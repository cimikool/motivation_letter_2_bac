Subject: Application for the Post of Part-Time Assistant Teacher


Dear Sir / Madam,


OpeningI am writing to apply for the position of part-time assistant teacher advertised recently at your centre. I believe I have the qualities and skills required for this role.


QualificationsI am currently a second-year Baccalaureate student at Abdellah Guennoun High School, where I have achieved good results in English and other subjects. In addition, I have some experience helping younger students in my neighbourhood with their English homework.


MotivationI am enthusiastic, responsible, and patient. I am particularly interested in this position because I hope to pursue a career in education in the future. I am confident that I would contribute positively to your centre and to your students' progress.


ClosingI would be grateful if you could consider my application. I am available for an interview at your convenience and I look forward to hearing from you.


Yours faithfully,

Fatima-Zahra Berrada

Opening paragraph — Purpose

State clearly why you are writing. Mention the job/programme and where you saw it.
→ "I am writing to apply for…"

Body §1 — Qualifications & Experience

Mention your level of study, academic results, and any relevant experience. Use In addition to add information.
→ "I am currently a student at… I have achieved…"

Body §2 — Personal Qualities & Motivation

Describe two or three personal qualities. Explain WHY you want this job or programme. Show enthusiasm.
→ "I am enthusiastic… I am particularly interested because…"

Closing paragraph — Request

Ask for an interview or a reply. Express availability. Close politely.
→ "I would be grateful if… I look forward to hearing from you."

Layout Checklist

✔ Sender's address — top right  |  ✔ Date below address  |  ✔ Recipient's address — left

✔ Subject line  |  ✔ Dear Sir / Madam,  |  ✔ Yours faithfully / sincerely + name

🎙 Concept Check Questions
Ask students: "Can I write 'I'm really excited'? Why not?" → elicit: contractions and informal words are not appropriate in formal letters.

Grammar Highlights in the Model

Find these structures in the letter and underline them:

1. Present Simple — to state facts & qualities
"I am currently a student…" / "I have experience…"

2. Modal verbs — polite and professional tone
"I would be grateful…" / "I would contribute…"

3. Passive construction — formal style
"…advertised recently at your centre"

4. Infinitive of purpose — expressing goals
"…I hope to pursue a career…"

5. Connectors of addition
"In addition," / "Furthermore," / "Moreover,"

Error Hunt — Find & Fix the Mistakes

Each sentence has ONE mistake common for Moroccan learners. Click "Show answer" to check.

1. "I'm writing to apply for the job advertised in the newspaper."
✔ "I am writing…" — No contractions in formal letters.
2. "I think I would be good at this post."
✔ "I am confident that I would be suitable for this post." — "I think" is too informal.
3. "I am very interested by English teaching."
✔ "interested in English teaching." — The correct preposition is "in".
4. "Waiting for your reply, accept my best greetings."
✔ "I look forward to hearing from you. Yours faithfully," — Avoid French-influenced closings.
5. "I am a serious and punctual worker, furthermore I am patient."
✔ "…punctual worker. Furthermore, I am patient." — Use a full stop before "Furthermore", then a comma after it.
